Scholarship Details

Application Procedure

+

To apply for the Deutschlandstipendium at University of Heidelberg, students must:

  • Be enrolled or admitted to a degree program at (Bachelor’s, Master’s, or Staatsexamen).
  • Apply online through the HU Berlin Deutschlandstipendium portal during the designated application period.
  • Submit required documents such as academic transcripts, proof of social or voluntary engagement, and a motivational statement.

The application period for the 2026/27 funding year is expected to open in October 2025. Specific dates are announced annually on the HU Berlin website.

Selection & Evaluation Criteria

+

Applications are evaluated based on a combination of:

  • Outstanding academic achievements
  • Demonstrated commitment to social causes, volunteer work, or extracurricular activities
  • Personal background and potential barriers overcome (e.g., first-generation students, migration background)

The evaluation process aims to identify well-rounded candidates who show both academic excellence and societal engagement.

Amount and Benefits of the Scholarship

+

The Deutschlandstipendium offers:

  • A fixed monthly stipend of €300 for one year (12 months)
  • No impact on BAföG (federal student aid) or taxation
  • Opportunities to connect with sponsors and attend networking events

The scholarship is renewable annually, subject to continued academic performance and engagement.

Course Attendance and Academic Standing

+

To maintain the Deutschlandstipendium, recipients must:

  • Remain enrolled in a full-time degree program
  • Demonstrate continued academic excellence and engagement
  • Inform the university of any changes in status or study interruptions

Non-compliance with academic or eligibility standards may result in loss of funding.

How to Apply

1

Choose a Degree Program

Select a Bachelor’s, Master’s, or Staatsexamen degree program at University of Heidelberg. Ensure you are either currently enrolled or admitted to begin in the upcoming semester.

2

Check Eligibility Criteria

Review the eligibility requirements for the Deutschlandstipendium, including academic excellence and voluntary/social engagement. Both German and international students can apply.

3

Gather Application Documents

Prepare documents such as transcripts, certificates, CV, motivation letter, and evidence of social engagement or personal hardship (if applicable).

4

Submit Application Online

Apply via the Deutschlandstipendium portal during the official application period (expected October 2025 for the 2026/27 cycle). Complete the form and upload all required documents.
